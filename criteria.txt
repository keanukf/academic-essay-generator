## Document Structure Requirements

The essay must follow this exact structure:
- Title page (thesis title, type, course name, course of study, date, author, matriculation number, tutor)
- Table of contents
- List of figures/tables (if applicable)
- List of abbreviations (if applicable)
- Text body: Introduction (~20%), Main Body (~70%), Conclusion (~10%)
- Bibliography
- Appendices (if applicable)

## Formatting Requirements

- Paper size: A4
- Margins: 2cm on all sides
- Font: Arial 11pt (body), Arial 12pt (headings), Arial 10pt (footnotes)
- Line spacing: 1.5
- Text alignment: Justified with hyphenation
- Paragraph spacing: 6pt after line breaks
- Section numbering: Maximum three levels (1. / 1.1 / 1.1.1)
- Page numbering: Roman numerals for front matter, Arabic for body text

## Evaluation Criteria (Weighted)

### 1. Introduction (4% weight)
The introduction must:
- Provide rationale for the topic from current state of research
- Clearly state the aim and research question
- Define scope and delimitation of the topic
- Include topic-related definitions where necessary
- Present an overview of structure and argumentation sequence
- Establish relevance by identifying open questions in the field

### 2. Structure (12% weight)
The essay must:
- Follow a logical progression of main points and explanatory points
- Be coherent and cohesive as a whole
- Follow a linear structure with clear transitions between sections
- Logically integrate main and explanatory points across sections
- Use transitions that reference key arguments from previous chapters
- Have each paragraph begin with a topic sentence
- Connect supporting points clearly to topic sentences
- Conclude arguments in ways that lead to the next point

### 3. Theory (20% weight)
The essay must:
- Demonstrate thorough literature evaluation
- Show application and understanding of key definitions
- Present a clear theoretical approach
- Use high-quality academic sources (peer-reviewed journals, seminal works)
- Avoid assuming any result or position to be true without evidence
- Support or counter positions with reliable sources and facts
- Explain anything not considered common knowledge with theory-based arguments

### 4. Methodology (20% weight)
The essay must:
- Provide clear information on the chosen methodology
- Justify why the methodology is appropriate for the research question
- Demonstrate appropriate use of methods for the topic
- Balance theoretical and practical/reflective components
- Explain procedures for dealing with the research question
- Show awareness of methodological limitations

### 5. Argumentation (20% weight)
The essay must:
- Present high-quality argumentation with clear key structure
- Demonstrate clarity of reasoning throughout
- Show comprehensive topic comprehension
- Combine theory and practical application effectively
- Include critical assessment of findings
- Provide own interpretation supported by evidence
- Prove and discuss all assumptions, considerations, and arguments
- Maintain a common thread linking each key point

### 6. Conclusion (4% weight)
The conclusion must:
- Give a final, overall impression (not repetition of content)
- Draw arguments to a close
- Summarize key arguments
- Answer the research question raised in the introduction
- Include follow-up questions or future research perspectives
- NOT introduce new ideas or arguments

### 7. Representation (4% weight)
The essay must:
- Present information clearly and accessibly
- Use appropriate academic formatting
- Include well-designed figures/tables if applicable
- Maintain consistent formatting throughout

### 8. Accuracy (4% weight)
The essay must:
- Be free of spelling errors
- Use correct punctuation
- Follow consistent formatting conventions

### 9. Language (4% weight)
The essay must:
- Use appropriate academic linguistic expression
- Maintain formal academic tone throughout
- Avoid colloquialisms and informal language
- Use precise terminology consistently
- Write in clear, well-constructed sentences

### 10. Literature/Bibliography (8% weight)
The essay must:
- Include properly formatted references
- Use consistent citation style throughout
- Cite all sources used in the text
- Include only sources actually referenced in the essay
- Prioritize recent and authoritative sources

## Quality Thresholds for Review Agent

The Review Agent should evaluate each criterion on a scale of 0.0 to 1.0 and flag for revision if:
- Any criterion scores below 0.6
- Overall weighted score falls below 0.7
- Theory, Methodology, or Argumentation (the 20% criteria) score below 0.7

## Word Count Targets

- Total text body: 5000-6000 words (12-15 pages)
- Introduction: ~1000-1200 words (~20%)
- Main Body: ~3500-4200 words (~70%)
- Conclusion: ~500-600 words (~10%)

## Critical Success Factors

1. Research question must be clearly stated and consistently addressed
2. All claims must be supported by cited evidence
3. Argumentation must follow logical progression
4. Theory and practice must be integrated, not separated
5. Critical analysis must go beyond description
6. Transitions must create cohesive narrative flow
7. Conclusion must answer the research question definitively
